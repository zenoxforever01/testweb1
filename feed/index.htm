Delanie’s newborn session

I’ve known Delanie’s mom for a while now. We met a few years back… I started photographing her oldest daughter Natalie when she was about 6 months old and then little brother Blake came along. Not too long after we did one of Blake’s milestone pictures, Megan told me that another little princess was arriving soon! I was sooo excited and we started texting ideas back and forth right away.

In the last few years, Megan started a new business. She custom designs invitations, announcements, wedding place cards and more… and she seriously does the most beautiful and creative work! It literally takes me forever to design a birth announcement, (I used to use templates that I bought online), but now Megan is my go to person for these. So whenever you order a birth announcement through me, she will design your custom card based on the images you choose. Everyone should be ordering these in my opinion, they are a one of a kind keepsake! You can go check out some of her beautiful work here: Megan Beaudrie Designs

I’m just so grateful for the wonderful friendships this business has brought me. I love that I will get a random text from Megan with an updated picture of my work up on her walls, letting me know how much she appreciates it, so much so that it made her cry.

Owning your own business can definitely be stressful and overwhelming at times. There have been so many times I’ve felt like quitting over the years.  I’ve played the comparison game and felt as if my work wasn’t good enough, and I’ve also been completely out of balance at times. I’m telling you this because whenever I have felt that way, I always come back to the good moments,  like these little gestures from people that help keep me going. I get to document these sweet babies during their first days and create memories for their family to cherish. I AM making a difference, and I’m doing exactly what I was meant to.

Flashes of Hope | Ann Arbor Michigan

A couple of months ago an organization called Flashes of Hope reached out to me asking if I would be interested in volunteering for an upcoming photoshoot for them. In case you aren’t familiar with them, Flashes of Hope is a non profit organization that provides photography to families with children fighting cancer. They also raise money for pediatric cancer research.

I don’t really have much experience transporting my equipment to shoot on location like this, so that part made me a bit nervous because I wanted to be sure to deliver the same quality of images that I do from my studio, but I knew I really wanted to do this, so I said yes and put the date on my calendar. This shoot was just a couple of weeks before the covid pandemic got really bad and we were issued a stay at home order here in Michigan (more on that later)

The concept of the photoshoot is simple, a pop up studio is set up in the hospital (we used a waiting room) It’s a break for the kids in between treatments, or just a chance to get out of their room, it gives them a few moments to forget about being in the hospital environment for a bit and have a little fun. These are also treasured moments for parents who receive several black and white images from the shoot in both digital and print. The organization also provided a little gift bag to the kids after their shoot as well, which I thought was really awesome.

Upon getting started I expected that some of the kids might not to feel like smiling for pictures due to not feeling well, but it was actually quite the opposite. Even though they are fighting a battle bigger than some of us can imagine, they were still in the best spirits, the little ones were playful and the smiles were so big and contagious. I photographed 14 children from 2 months old up to 16, and they were all amazing and inspiring. I am so thankful to have had the opportunity to meet these brave kids and their families.

Today is April 4th, 2020. We’re about 2 weeks in to this whole stay at home isolation thing. I’ve had my down moments for sure, but then I think of these kids and their spirits when facing something so serious and scary and it inspires me to be better, to try to be a positive light in any situation.

If you would like to read more about this organization, donate, or if you would be interested in volunteering, you can check out their website here: https://flashesofhope.org
